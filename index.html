About Yoga

In the western part of the world, if you utter the word “Yoga,” people think of impossible physical postures. This is a very distorted idea of what Yoga is. Yoga is not about bending and twisting your body or holding your breath. It is a mechanism and a technology to get you to that state of experience where you see reality just the way it is. The word “Yoga” literally means “union.”

Modern science is proving to you that the whole existence is just one energy. The religions of the world have been saying for a long time that God is everywhere. Whether you say, “God is everywhere,” or you say, “Everything is one energy,” we are talking about the same reality. It is just that a scientist has never experienced this – he has arrived at it through mathematical deductions; a religious person has not experienced it – he believes it because it is written somewhere or has been said by someone.

Asanas



Trikonasana


There are more than three variations available for this pose. Let us begin with variation one first.

Step 1
Stand erect with feet three feet apart.

Step 2
Raise the arms and keep them horizontally on both sides so that they are parallel to the floor.

Step 3
Bend on the right side and place the right hand on the right foot.

Step 4
Turn the head and fix eyesight on the left palm. Breathe normally. Keep the position as long as it is comfortable.

Step 5
Release the pose and come back to the standing position. Repeat the process on the other side.


Uttanasna


Step 1
Stand erect with feet together and arms on the sides of the body. Breathe normally.

Step 2
While exhaling, bend forward, bending the head first.

Step 3
Then bend the upper trunk and finally the lower trunk.

Step 4
Place the palms on the floor. Touch the knees with your forehead.

step5
The legs and arms should be straight. Breathe Normally. Keep the position as long as it is comfortable.

Dhanurasana

Step 1
Lie on your stomach

Step 2
Hold your both feet with your hands making a back bend and positioning like a bow.

Step 3
Pull your both feet slowly – slowly, as much as you can.

Look straight ahead with a smile in your face .

Step 5
Keep the pose stable while paying attention to your breath. After 1-20 seconds as you exhale,gently bring your legs and chest to the ground and relax.

Paschimottanasana

Step 1
Sit with legs outstretched . Then, straighten your torso to keep the spine erect. Take a deep breath and expand the chest.

Step 2
While exhaling, bend forward from the hips without making a curve of your lower spine. After that, try to catch the big toes with your thumb and fingers.

Step 3
After that, Inhale, keeping your legs straightened by your arms without giving any pressure to your back.

Step 4
Finally, release the position and repeat the steps five times
